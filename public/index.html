Industry Overview

A New Era for Technical Ceramics

Ceramic 3D printing — spanning direct ink writing (DIW), lithography-based ceramic manufacturing (LCM), binder jetting, and digital light processing (DLP) — is transforming how industries produce complex oxide and non-oxide parts.

From aerospace thermal protection systems to same-day chairside dental restorations, ceramic AM is expanding what hard, brittle materials can do.

Key Applications

Where Ceramic 3D Printing Excels

Industrial & Thermal

Heat exchangers, kiln furniture, combustion liners, and refractory components with geometries impossible to mold.

Design & Tableware

Studio pottery, bespoke tableware, and decorative objects with algorithmic surface textures and parametric forms.

Architecture & Facades

Custom glazed facade panels, acoustic tiles, and structural terracotta elements at architectural scale.

Biomedical

Hydroxyapatite scaffolds for bone regeneration, dental crowns, and patient-specific surgical implants.
